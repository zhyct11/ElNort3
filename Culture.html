Culture of Ilocos Norte

Pamulinawen

An annual cultural festival in Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It is held in January to celebrate the rich cultural heritage of Ilocos Norte, particularly its indigenous people known as the Ilocanos. The festival features a parade of colorful costumes, street dancing, and traditional music and dances, as well as exhibits showcasing the local products and crafts. The word "Pamulinawen" is derived from the Ilocano word "pamulinaw" which means "to be beautiful." The festival aims to promote the rich cultural heritage of Ilocos Norte, and to attract tourists to the area.

Culture of Ilocos Norte

Ani Festival

The Ani Festival is a yearly occasion during March in the region of Dingras. Not at all like most Philippine celebrations that celebrate for the sake of their Patron Sain. Ani celebration fills in as a thanksgiving festivity of Dingras ranchers for their plentiful grounds and rich harvests. By and large, the festival begins on Sunday and closures on a Friday. It is one of the exceedingly foreseen celebrations in Ilocos Norte alongside Burgos Town Festival, Dulang Food Festival, Calesa Festival, Gameng Festival, Mannalon Festival, and others.
